### Before the interview

- **Re-read your own mistakes from this session**, not new material. You already know the concepts; what you need is the precise vocabulary (exception names, pattern names, mechanism names). Skimming your own corrected answers the morning of is more valuable than learning anything new.
- **Prepare 2-3 questions to ask them** — about the codebase, team structure, how they handle deployments/on-call, or what a typical sprint looks like. Not having questions reads as disinterest, and it's an easy, low-effort thing to prep.
- **Have a short, clean answer ready for "walk me through your experience"** — this always opens technical interviews. Practice it once out loud so it doesn't come out rambly.

### During technical questions (the mindset that matters most)
- **Say your reasoning before you say your answer.**
- If you don't know something, say what you _do_ know that's adjacent, then say clearly "I'm not certain on the exact mechanism, but my understanding is roughly..."
- **Ask clarifying questions before diving into code.** For live coding: constraints, edge cases (empty array? negative numbers? duplicates?), expected complexity. This is expected, not a weakness.
- **State brute force first, always**, then say "but I can optimize this by..."
- **Talk while you code.**
